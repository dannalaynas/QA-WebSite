YUBA + Companies

Preparing

Your time at Year Up Bay Area (YUBA) is divided in half. The first phase is building the foundation for students. Teaching them all the skills they will need to make a smooth transition into the coporate world. All phases consisting of a Business Communications and Pro Skills course. All tech courses are constantly changing throughout a 3 week period; excluding the last moduel. Courses such as Network + System Admin, Web Development, IT Foundations, and IT HelpDesk. The second phase consiting of the 6 month period were each intern is sent to work for one of the corporate partners. All students are given on hands, on the job training. In other words the learning never stops. Interns are given tasks at their internships that help grow that foundations they built in the first phase of Year Up.

Learning + Development

You propbably saw me mention something about a moduel in the previous section. The first phase of Year Up, also known as the Learning and Development (L&D) phase, is split up into 3 moduels. The first moduel is when students are introduced to the tech fundamentals considering that a handfull of the students don't come from a technical background. The second moduel is where students get to dive in deeper to the tech material; learning the Hardware and Software side. At the end of the second moduel students are tested on everything they've learned since the beginning of program. The IRA (Internship Readiness Assesment) takes place for a week. Students demonstrate why they deserve an internship. They go threw a variety of tasks such as mock interviews, students are required to create a website portfolio displaying all their skills and work they've created throughout the program, and students are require to go threw a series of tech scenerios that they demonstrate for instructors. Once IRA week is over it marks the beginning of moduel 3. Moduel 3 is when students are placed into their tracks. Students use up the remaining month to focus on one area of experties. Weather it be QA, Project Cordination, Network + System Admin, Cyber Security, or IT HelpDesk. Students get the oportunity to dive into detail about their tracks.
